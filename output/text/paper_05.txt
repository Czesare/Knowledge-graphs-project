4. Conclusionsandfuturework
Inthispaper,wehaveintroducedandmotivatedtheneedforhybrid-autonomyinaso-
cial window that, on the one hand, directs research towards increasingly sophisticated
autonomy and technology [8] - and, on the other hand, legitimately demands control
overadvancedsystemsandrespectfortheirethicalandmoralvalues[31].Buildingon

both at the epistemological and applicative stages. By embedding these principles, we
aim to support the design of systems that not only meet technical performance bench-
marks,butalsoalignwithusers’expectationswithregardtotheinteraction.Basingon
the commitment and attention these contributions call to the development of more fair
AI-powered autonomous systems, our work is addressing a specific need by applying

and address ethical adaptability and trust factors. By synthesising and harmonising in-

Future work will focus on validating this proposal across diverse domains, exploring
practicalimplementationspossibilitiesthroughsurveysanduserstudies,andaddressing
challengesintheinterplaybetweenhumansandautonomoussystems.
