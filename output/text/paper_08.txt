1. Introduction
Whenlarge-scalecrisiseventsoccur,atimelysituationalawarenessfacilitatestheiden-
tificationofresponse actions. Alargevolumeofpotentially valuabledataisgenerated,
forexample,onsocialmedia.However,methodsforisolatingrelevant andinformative

drift [2]. In order to update the available knowledge, understanding which data should
beverifiedbecomesthenakeychallenge.Tomitigatetheimplicationsofconceptdrift,
an Active Learning strategy (AL) can be employed to update the predictor with recent,
informative samples. The aim of this paper is to explore the challenge of extracting a

Selectionstrategiesareusuallybasedoninformation-basedfeatures,suchasuncer-

Information quality (IQ) indicators, which are valuable for situational awareness, are
usually overlooked by AL selection strategies. In this work, we then explore the con-
tribution of IQ indicators to characterizing data during an emergency [4]. To evaluate

2. Relatedwork
Many efforts have been dedicated to extracting relevant data during emergency events.
Atthesametime,emergenciesarecharacterizedbysignificantinstabilityduetorapidly
unfolding events. Surprisingly, only a small amount of work has focused on the task
of extracting relevant data with an AL framework [5,6] or as a few-shot setting [7] in
emergency scenarios, especially since adaptation to concept drift has been extensively
studied [8] and the tailoring of AL methods to context has emerged as a requirement
[9]. Several AL approaches try to maximize the efficiency of labelling in pool-based
[10]andstreamingscenarios[11].Amongtheproposedapproaches,information-based
strategies leverage predictor properties, such as their uncertainty, by selecting the most
uncertainitemsaccordingtothecurrentpredictor[12],ortheirdisagreement,byselect-
ing items for which the disagreement among different models is largest [13]. In con-
trast,representation-basedstrategiesexploitthedensityoftheinputspaceinaneffortto
selectrepresentativepoints[14].Akeylimitationofinformation-basedmethodsisthat
theytendtofocusonpointsnearthedecisionboundaries,neglectingthedistributionof
thedata.Ontheotherhand,representation-basedapproachesoftenneedmorequeriesto
adequatelycovertheinputspace,leadingtoslowerconvergence[3].Someauthorshave
proposed to directly measure the expected contribution of individual data points to the
performanceofapredictor,forexample,usingestimatesofShapleyvalues[15].
Tothebestofourknowledge,noinvestigationhasbeenconductedonthepotential
ofIQindicatorsfortheALtask.Theimpactofdataqualityandtheircontextualnature
have been extensively studied[16]. Data quality on social media data has also been fo-
cusedon[17],andrelatedIQindicatorsframeworkshavebeenproposedforemergency
scenarios[4].However,theirimpactintermsofadaptationremainsmainlyunstudiedin
thisscenario.

5. Conclusionsandfuturework
Thisstudyhighlightsexploratorybutencouragingresultsontheuseofinformationqual-
ityindicatorsforanalyzingsocialmediadataduringemergencies.Theseindicatorscap-
ture salient characteristics of data, contributing to the individuation of relevant items
and ultimately enhancing context awareness. Notably, indicator importances are time-
sensitive and context-bound, underscoring the importance of their dynamic evaluation
as events unfold. We show that the IQ space can effectively support Active Learning
(AL) with consistent behaviour and low budgets. We also show that the importance of
dataitemsisconsistentovertime,eveniffurtherresearchisneededtofullyharnessthis
information,possiblycontributingtothequantificationoftheevolutiondynamic,which
couldbelinkedtotheALtask,forexample,byparameterizingtheselectionstrategyQ .

A current limitation of this study is a systematic exploration of fine-grained win-
dowingapproaches,whichwillbeaddressedinfuturework.Thesameholdsfortheab-
sence of a streaming-based setup, which would better reflect real-world use cases and
facilitatemeaningfulcomparisons.Moreover,theresultsarelimitedtotheproposedset
of indicators and scenarios. The method utilized in Section 4.4 does not scale well to
largerdatasets,highlightingtheneedforfurtherexplorationofheuristicsaimedatdata-
intensivescenarios.
