1. Introductionandliteraturereview
Multimodal learning is an essential area of Artificial Intelligence (AI), enabling mod-
els to integrate and process diverse data sources such as images, text, audio, and sen-
sor readings. These models are widely applied in fields including autonomous driving
[1],healthcare[2],emotionrecognition[3],andhuman-computerinteraction[4,5].Mul-
timodality mirrors the human ability to synthesize information from multiple sensory
inputs,enhancingperceptionandinteraction.
Despite its advantages, multimodal learning faces significant challenges in real-
world applications due to missing data or missing modalities, which can arise from
sensor failures, occlusions, transmission errors, or adversarial conditions. The absence
of critical modalities during training or inference can severely degrade model perfor-
mance.Tomitigatethis,recentadvancesinMultimodalLearningwithMissingModali-
ties(MLMM)haveexploredtechniquestoenhancemodelrobustness[6].
Various approaches have been proposed to address these challenges, including
shared-specific feature modeling to separate shared and modality-specific information
[7], parameter-efficient adaptation to refine pre-trained models with minimal overhead
[8], and Bayesian meta-learning to enable adaptation in extreme missing modality
scenarios [9]. Another emerging method is multimodal prompt learning, which uses
modality-aware prompts to guide inference and maintain accuracy even with missing
inputs[10][11].
These studies highlight the importance of assessing the robustness of multimodal
modelsinthepresenceofincompleteinputs.Inthiscontext,robustnessisadefiningtrait
of high-quality AI, reflected not only in performance under ideal conditions, but in the
model’sabilitytoremainreliablewhenfacedwithdatavariabilityordegradation.
One promising direction to support such robustness lies in architectural designs
thatcancapturecomplementaryinformationacrossmodalities,whilealsoexploitingre-
dundancy to compensate for potential data loss. In this context, Multi-Head Networks
(MHNs) provide a flexible framework in which multiple parallel processing heads in-
dependentlyextractdiversefeaturerepresentations.Originallyintroducedinthecontext
ofself-attentionmechanisms[12],theyhavesincebecomeacorecomponentinvarious
multimodal applications, including vision-language modeling [13], audio-visual event
localization[14],andcross-modalretrieval[15].Beyondattention-basedmodels,multi-
head architectures have also been successfully applied in multi-task learning [16], ob-
ject detection[17], and other deeplearning frameworks to promotemodularity, feature
diversity,andgeneralization.
In this study, we extend the MHN paradigm by designing architectures in which
each head is an independently parameterized sub-network operating on the same in-
put.Topromoterepresentationaldiversityandrobustness,weintroduceanoveltraining
lossthatincorporatesanorthogonality-basedregularizationterm.Thistermencourages
decorrelationamongthelatentrepresentationsproducedbydifferentheads,withtheaim
ofenhancingrobustnesstomissinginformation.
Wetestedthreearchitecturevariants,m-FFNet,m-ConvNet,andm-LSTMNet,each
correspondingtoadifferentfeatureextractionstrategy.Inallcases,theprefixmdenotes

2. Methodology
This section describes the methodology employed in designing and evaluating the pro-
posed models and loss function. The work integrates multiple neural network architec-
tures to extract features from input data and applies a novel loss function to improve
classificationperformancethroughanorthogonalityconstraintonlatentrepresentations.
Theneworthogonalitylossisspecificallydesignedtomitigateperformancedropsinsce-
narios where certain inputs are missing. While the primary task remains classification,
theadditionoftheorthogonalitylossenhancestherobustnessofthemodelagainstinput
perturbations,ensuringmorestablefeaturerepresentations.
The idea of enforcing orthogonality to promote feature diversity or separation has
beenexploredinpreviousworksforotherpurposes[18,19].However,unliketheseprior
studies, our orthogonality constraint is specifically adapted to multi-head architectures
topromoteresilienceagainstmissingmodalitiesinmultimodallearning.

4. Conclusionsandperspectiveoutlooks
ThisstudyinvestigatestheintegrationofMulti-HeadNetwork(MHN)architectureswith
orthogonality-based regularization as a strategy to enhance robustness against missing
modalityfeaturesinmultimodallearning.Ourresultsshowthatthedegreeofimprove-
mentisarchitecture-dependent,withconvolutionalmodelsbenefitingthemostfromthe
proposedapproach.
Tofurthervalidateourapproach,futureresearchwillextendevaluationtoadditional
datasetsandapplicationdomains,aimingtoassessitsadaptabilityindiversemultimodal
contexts. A refined training and testing pipeline is also under development: after train-
ing MHNs with heterogeneous heads, explainability techniques will identify the most
informativemodalities.Thiswillguidetheselectionandtuningofthenumberofheads
tomaximizerobustnesswhileminimizingperformancevariabilitywhencriticalfeatures
aremissing.
The code for our experiments will be made publicly available to foster further re-
searchanddevelopmentinmultimodalAI.
